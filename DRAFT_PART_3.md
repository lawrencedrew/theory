# Part III: The Pandemonium (Entities and Zones)

## Chapter 6: The 10 Zones and the 45 Demons

The Numogram is not empty. It is heavily populated. This population forms the **Pandemonium Matrix**, a catalog of 55 entities: 10 Zones and 45 Demons (or "Lemurs").

Each of the 10 Zones is a distinct territory with its own intensive quality. 
Zone 1 (Lurgo) is the initiator; Zone 4 (Krako) is burning fatality; Zone 5 (Katak) is cataclysmic combustion; Zone 9 (Ummnu) is ultimate inconsequence. 

Connecting these Zones are the 45 Demons. They are not entities with horns and pitchforks; they are "net-spans," functional bridges or traffic-gates that route the chaotic currents of the Labyrinth. They are mathematically defined by their "Mesh Number" (00 to 44) and their "Net-Span" (the two zones they connect).

*   **Mesh-00 (Lurgo):** Connects 1::0. The initiator of the Void.
*   **Mesh-14 (Katak):** Connects 5::4. The Syzygetic Chronodemon of the Plex, the engine of destruction.
*   **Mesh-18 (Djynxx):** Connects 6::3. The Syzygetic Xenodemon of the Warp, the agent of temporal synchronization.
*   **Mesh-36 (Uttunul):** Connects 9::0. The Syzygetic Xenodemon of the Seething Void.

Demons are categorized by their function: **Chronodemons** regulate flow within the Time-Circuit; **Amphidemons** bridge the gaps between the Time-Circuit, the Warp, and the Plex; and **Xenodemons** belong entirely to the alien domains of Outer-Time.

To practice Lemurian Time-Sorcery is to calculate the pathways between Zones, effectively "summoning" these demons to route your intent through the machinic unconscious.

## Chapter 7: The Cthulhu-Matrix

The Pandemonium system inevitably collides with the cosmic horror of H.P. Lovecraft, forming the **Cthulhu-Matrix**. The CCRU recognized that Lovecraft was not merely a fiction writer, but an unconscious channeler of hyperstitional architecture.

The map aligns perfectly:
*   **The Outer Zones (0, 9 - The Warp):** This is the domain of Yog-Sothoth and the Outer Gods—the absolute, incomprehensible outside that surrounds our fragile reality. It is the creeping chaos of Nyarlathotep (Zones 3, 6).
*   **The Plex (4, 5):** This is the terrestrial depth, the sunken city of R'lyeh. It is the domain of Cthulhu and the Deep Ones (Zone 8 - Mur Mur). It is the intensive heat and pressure of "Cthelll," the earth's inner core.

When you traverse the Numogram, you are navigating the very architecture of cosmic horror. You are moving past the veil of human sanity and interfacing directly with the Old Ones—not as mythological beasts, but as hyperstitional vectors of time and intensity.
